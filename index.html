About Me

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Applied and Computational Mathematics and Statistics at the University of Notre Dame, with a concurrent appointment in the Department of Aerospace and Mechanical Engineering. My research focuses on the development of robust and scalable computational methods for uncertainty quantification, data assimilation, machine learning, and agentic scientific computing.

A primary application area of my work is in cardiovascular modeling, where we aim to create patient-specific simulations to improve diagnosis, treatment planning, and medical device design. My lab develops techniques for multi-fidelity surrogate modeling, Bayesian inference, and integrating physics-based models with clinical data from electronic health records and medical imaging.
